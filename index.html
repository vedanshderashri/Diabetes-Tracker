Understanding Insulin

Insulin Basics

Insulin is a hormone produced by the pancreas that allows glucose to enter your cells to produce energy. In Type 1 Diabetes, the body produces no insulin. In Type 2, the body is resistant to it.

Common Medications

Oral Anti-Diabetics & Metformin

Metformin is commonly the first medication recommended for Type 2. It helps improve body sensitivity to insulin. General side effects include mild stomach discomfort or nausea. Do not stop prescribed medicines without a doctor.

Diagnostic Guide

Glucose Target Ranges

Standard target levels: Fasting should be under 100 mg/dL for non-diabetics, 100-125 for prediabetics, and 126+ indicates diabetes. Postprandial targets are ideally under 140 mg/dL (under 180 mg/dL for diabetics).

Dietary Principles

Glycemic Index (GI) Controls

Prefer low Glycemic Index foods like steel-cut oats, green leafy vegetables, lentils, and kidney beans that trigger slow sugar absorption. Avoid white rice, refined bread, and sugar-laden juices.
